What is a Dividend Payer?

A dividend is a small portion of a company's profits that it gives back to its shareholders as a reward for owning its stock. A "Dividend Payer" is simply any company that does this. The amount can change, and they can stop paying at any time. It's a good sign, but it doesn't guarantee future reliability.

What is a Dividend Aristocrat?

This is a more exclusive club. To become a Dividend Aristocrat, a company must be part of the S&P 500 index (a list of 500 of the largest U.S. companies) and must have increased its dividend payment every single year for at least 25 consecutive years. This shows incredible financial stability and a strong commitment to shareholders, even through economic recessions.

What is a Dividend King?

This is the highest honor in the dividend world. A Dividend King is a company that has increased its dividend payment every single year for at least 50 consecutive years. This is an extraordinary track record of reliability and growth, proving the company has a durable business model that has thrived through half a century of market changes, technological shifts, and economic cycles.
